Topics of Interest

The IARML workshop gathers researchers from machine learning, natural language processing, knowledge representation, and cognitive modeling to explore intersections between analogical and commonsense reasoning. We particularly encourage innovative contributions that bridge theory and practice. We welcome submissions addressing, but not limited to, the following themes:

Analogical Reasoning and Cognitive Modeling:

  • Cognitive theories and computational models of analogical reasoning
  • Analogy and abstraction mechanisms
  • Analogy and conceptual metaphor understanding
  • Figurative language processing, sarcasm, irony, and analogy
  • Empirical cognitive or psychological studies on human analogical reasoning
  • Cognitive frameworks and psychological validation of analogical processes

Machine Learning Methods for Analogical Reasoning:

  • Representation learning and embedding techniques for analogies
  • Advanced similarity measures and their role in analogy
  • Transfer learning and analogy-based generalization
  • Neuro-symbolic AI architectures for analogical inference
  • Extracting and structuring analogies from large knowledge bases

Analogical Reasoning Enhancing Machine Learning:

  • Analogical approaches to classification and clustering
  • Case-Based Reasoning methods enhanced by analogies
  • Creativity, data augmentation, and generative models using analogical approaches
  • Verification and augmentation of large language and vision models through analogy

Analogies in Large AI Models (LLMs and Vision Models):

  • Probing and benchmarking analogical reasoning capabilities in large models
  • Evaluation frameworks and novel tasks assessing analogies in language and vision
  • Studies of analogical reasoning abilities in large language models and visual diffusion models
  • Analogical abstraction in generative AI

Tasks, Benchmarks, and Evaluation:

  • Novel benchmarks and datasets for evaluating analogical reasoning
  • Matching and aligning narratives, situational descriptions, or structured knowledge
  • Analogy understanding and generation in longer formats (narratives, dialogues, videos)
  • Visual analogy tasks, including abstraction and interpretation
  • Analogical discovery for computational creativity and scientific innovation

Applications and Impact of Analogical Reasoning:

  • Analogical methods for personalization, recommendation, and explanation
  • Educational applications and pedagogical implications of analogy
  • Innovations driven by analogical reasoning in science, technology, and engineering
  • Analogical reasoning applications in law, healthcare, software engineering, and knowledge management
  • Studies of societal, ethical, or practical impacts of deploying analogical reasoning systems
